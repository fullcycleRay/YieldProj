Summary

Ascend Real Estate Partners (“Originator”), together with YieldStreet, is closing shortly on a mezzanine loan for an aggregate amount of $55M (the “mezzanine loan”). The mezzanine loan has a $40M senior tranche (“mezzanine loan A”), in which YieldStreet is investing $15M, as well as a subordinated $15M junior tranche (“mezzanine loan B”). YieldStreet’s investment represents 37.5% of the mezzanine loan A and 27.3% of the aggregate mezzanine loan; the Originator and its investor syndicate will hold the remainder.

Proceeds from the mezzanine loan will be used in conjunction with a $70M bank bridge loan to finance a high-profile developer’s (“Borrower”) $128M buyout of its financial partner’s ownership in a 16-story multifamily rental building in the heart of the Upper West Side, one of Manhattan’s premier neighborhoods (the “Property”). The Property was valued at $155M on an “As-Is” basis, resulting in a loan-to-value of 71.1% at close. The mezzanine loan is subordinated to the bank bridge loan and is secured by a pledge of the equity of the borrower.

Within 120 days of the mezzanine loan’s closing, the $70M bank bridge loan is required to be refinanced with a $100M senior bank loan. The Originator has advised YieldStreet that the Sponsor is expected to invest an additional $4.8M in equity to complete the renovations. The incremental proceeds from the refinancing and the additional equity investment will fund renovation expenses and interest reserves. The Borrower intends to convert the Property, currently operated as a rental building, to condominiums and sell them over a four-year period, using the proceeds to repay debt, including the mezzanine loan. The Originator expects, based on third-party studies, the sale of condominiums to yield $214M in gross proceeds. The maximum refinanced debt level of $140M (senior bank loan + mezzanine loan A) represents 65.3% loan-to-value ratio (“LTV”) based on the expected proceeds from the sale of the underlying condominiums.

YieldStreet investors have an opportunity to invest in borrower payment dependent notes (“BPDN”), the cash flow of which is dependent on the payment of principal and interest on the mezzanine loan. YieldStreet investors are scheduled to receive a monthly interest payment at a target annualized rate of 9.5% over the loan’s term. Of that target 9.5% rate, investors can expect to receive 8.0% in monthly cash interest payments and 1.5% in accrued interest, which are scheduled to be paid once principal repayment begins, expected to begin in month 32. The 1.5% interest accrued during the month will be paid-in-kind (“PIK”), i.e. added to the outstanding mezzanine loan balance, resulting in investors earning compounding interest (earning interest on the accrued interest). Please note that from initial close until the refinance of the bridge loan (contractually required within 120 days from close, but expected within 60 days), all interest payments will accrue, meaning there will be no cash interest payments to mezzanine loan investors, until the refinancing.

For more information on this opportunity, please review the information provided in the remaining sections of this summary and download the Series Note Supplement.

Note that for all offerings, investors are responsible for annual fund expenses. This offering is structured as a BPDN, as such, these expenses are $100 in year 1 and $30 in subsequent years. These are outlined in detail in the Series Note Supplement.

What Should I Consider?

Foreclosure Risk

The Property may need to be liquidated to recover funds in the event of a default.

Risk Mitigation

  • The Property has a closing LTV of 71.1% based on its appraised valuation of $155.0M, which means the net proceeds from foreclosing and selling the Property would have to return less than 71.1% for mezzanine loan A principal to be at risk.

Market Risk

The Borrower may be unable to sell the condominiums at the expected price due to a downward movement in the UWS condominium market.

Risk Mitigation

  • The realized sales price on the condominiums would have to decrease approximately 35% for the mezzanine loan A to experience a loss. During the Great Recession of 2008/09, the median sales price for condominiums on the UWS decreased by 21%.
  • In the event the UWS housing market experiences a material decline, historically, these periods have been relatively temporary. Despite decreasing by 21% in 2009, the median UWS condominium sales price increased 9% within a year.
  • The UWS neighborhood has strong underlying demographics, consisting of wealthy, permanent family- and community-oriented households. This helps stabilize valuations relative to other premium residential submarkets, which have experienced considerable development in recent years driven by more elastic demand from foreign investors.

Construction and Condominium Conversion Risk

The Borrower may be unable to convert the Property to condominium status in a reasonable timeframe and/or experience overruns in the cost or timing to complete the remaining renovations.

Risk Mitigation

  • The Borrower has experience in condominium conversions and is familiar with the required steps. Additionally, the Borrower has hired a real estate legal adviser to provide counsel on the process and time needed to complete the conversion.
  • The remaining renovation is relatively minimal and includes items such as upgrading amenities and replacing existing hallway flooring with marble and hardwood.
  • The two guarantors have provided a completion guaranty, which requires them to provide additional equity in the case of delays and/or cost overruns.

What Secures This Investment?

The mezzanine loan is secured by a pledge of the equity of the Borrower. At close, the mezzanine loan is subordinated to $70.2M in senior bank debt, which is secured by a mortgage on the property and has the first right to the Property. Upon a mezzanine loan default, including cross-default as a result of a default on the senior bank loan, the originator has the right to foreclose on the equity of the Borrower and take over the management of the Property, avoiding a forced liquidation at a discounted value. For more information on the structure, please refer to the Series Note Supplement. The Property was valued by Cushman & Wakefield, a leading real estate services company, “As-Is” at $155.0M, resulting in an LTV of 71.1% for the mezzanine loan A at close. The mezzanine loan A, in which YieldStreet SPV is a co-lender, benefits from subordinated capital of $15.0M in mezzanine loan B and $12M in Borrower equity in the transaction. As such, the net recovery of the Property can decrease up to 28.9% less before mezzanine loan A principal is impaired.

The Borrower intends to invest $20M in renovations and convert the Property to condominiums. The Property has 87 units which, after the conversion and renovations, are expected to generate sales proceeds of $214M. Based on the expected value of the condominiums, the mezzanine loan A has an LTV of 65.3%. In order for YieldStreet investors to incur principal loss, the sales price per square foot would need to decrease 35% to $1,450 from the Originator’s projected price of $2,300 per square foot.

While the Property is expected to generate rental income during the term of the mezzanine loan, the Originator has also required an interest reserve be funded upon refinancing of the bank bridge loan to service interest payments on all debt until condominium sales begin (expected in month 20). Combining the Property’s projected net operating income with the total interest reserves, debt service coverage is projected to be 1.1x during the months leading up to the start of condominium sales.

How Do I Get Paid?

Scheduled Payments

The mezzanine loan has full term of 60 months, consisting of a 36-month initial term and two 12-month extensions. YieldStreet investors are expected to receive monthly interest payments at a target annualized rate of 9.5%, 8.0% of which is in cash and 1.5% in accrued interest, which are scheduled to be paid once principal repayment begins, expected to begin in month 32. From the initial close of the mezzanine loan until the refinance of the bank bridge loan by the senior bank loan (contractually required within 120 days of close, but expected by the originator to be within 60 days of close), all interest payments will accrue, meaning there will be no cash interest payments to mezzanine loan investors, including YieldStreet investors, during such period.

The senior bank loan and mezzanine loan are structured such that only interest will be paid until condominium sales begin, which is expected in month 20. Proceeds from the sale of condominiums are first applied to accrued and cash interest payments with remaining cash flow used to first repay the senior bank debt, followed by the mezzanine loan A, and finally mezzanine loan B. The senior bank loan is expected to be repaid by month 32, the mezzanine loan A by month 40, and the mezzanine loan B by month 43.
